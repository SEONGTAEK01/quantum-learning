Quantum Mechanics Fundamentals

Wave-Particle Duality

The concept that light and matter simultaneously exhibit both wave and particle properties

References:

  • De Broglie wavelength (American Journal of Physics)
  • Louis de Broglie Nobel Lecture

Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

The principle that position and momentum cannot be simultaneously measured with perfect accuracy

Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ/2

References:

  • Heisenberg's Original Paper (1927)
  • Griffiths, D. J. "Introduction to Quantum Mechanics" Chapter 1

Quantum Superposition

The principle that quantum states can exist as linear combinations of multiple states

|ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩

References:

  • Nielsen & Chuang, "Quantum Computation and Quantum Information" Chapter 1
  • Quantum Country (Andy Matuschak)

Quantum Entanglement

The phenomenon where two or more particles become correlated such that measuring one instantly determines the state of others

Particle A
Particle B

References:

  • Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen Paradox (1935)
  • Bell's Theorem Experimental Verification

Famous Quantum Mechanics Experiments

Double-Slit Experiment

When electrons or photons pass through a double slit, they create an interference pattern. However, when we observe which slit they pass through, the interference pattern disappears.

Recommended Video:

Dr Quantum - Double Slit Experiment

Academic Sources:

  • Feynman, R. P. "The Feynman Lectures on Physics" Vol. III, Chapter 1
  • Modern version of double-slit experiment (American Journal of Physics)

Schrödinger's Cat

😸
💀
Superposition State

A thought experiment that illustrates the paradox of quantum superposition. The cat's life or death depends on radioactive decay, and until observed, it exists in a superposition of both states.

Original Paper:

  • Schrödinger, E. "Die gegenwärtige Situation in der Quantenmechanik" (1935)
  • Wheeler, J. A. & Zurek, W. H. "Quantum Theory and Measurement" Princeton University Press
